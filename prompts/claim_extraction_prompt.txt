You are a specialist in analyzing customer support conversations about damage claims. Your task is to extract precise, structured claim details from the conversation below.

=== CONVERSATION ===
{user_claim}

=== CLAIMED OBJECT TYPE ===
{claim_object}

=== YOUR TASK ===
Carefully read the entire conversation and extract the customer's FINAL stated damage claim. Conversations may be rambling, contradictory, or confusing — always use the LAST clearly stated version of the claim. If the customer revises their description partway through, use the revised version.

The conversation may contain text in multiple languages (Hindi, Spanish, Chinese, or other languages mixed with English). Translate and interpret all non-English text to understand the claim fully. Do not skip or ignore non-English segments — they may contain the actual claim details.

Extract the following fields:

1. **claimed_issue_type**: The type of damage or issue the customer is reporting.
   Allowed values (pick exactly one):
   - "dent" — a depression or indentation in a surface
   - "scratch" — a surface-level mark or scrape
   - "crack" — a fracture line in a rigid surface
   - "glass_shatter" — broken or shattered glass/screen
   - "broken_part" — a component that is broken, snapped, or non-functional
   - "missing_part" — a component that is absent or was not included
   - "torn_packaging" — packaging material that is ripped or torn
   - "crushed_packaging" — packaging that has been compressed or deformed
   - "water_damage" — damage caused by water, moisture, or liquid exposure
   - "stain" — a discoloration or mark from a substance
   - "none" — the customer does not describe any damage (use only if genuinely no damage is claimed)
   - "unknown" — the damage description is too vague or ambiguous to categorize

2. **claimed_object_part**: The specific part of the object that the customer says is damaged.
   Allowed values depend on the claim_object type:

   If claim_object is "car":
     front_bumper, rear_bumper, door, hood, windshield, side_mirror, headlight, taillight, fender, quarter_panel, body, unknown

   If claim_object is "laptop":
     screen, keyboard, trackpad, hinge, lid, corner, port, base, body, unknown

   If claim_object is "package":
     box, package_corner, package_side, seal, label, contents, item, unknown

   If claim_object does not match any of the above categories, use "unknown".

3. **damage_keywords**: A list of descriptive keywords the customer used to describe the damage.
   Extract the actual words/phrases they used (translated to English if originally in another language).
   Examples: ["deep scratch", "cracked screen", "dented hood", "water stain on the box"]
   Minimum 1 keyword, maximum 10. Be faithful to what the customer actually said.

4. **claimed_severity_description**: A brief summary (one phrase or sentence) of how severe the customer says the damage is, in their own words (translated to English if needed).
   If they don't explicitly describe severity, write "not explicitly stated".

5. **num_parts_claimed**: The number of distinct parts the customer claims are damaged.
   Count only clearly distinct parts. If they mention "the screen" multiple times, that is 1 part.
   If they mention "the screen and the keyboard", that is 2 parts.
   Must be an integer >= 0.

=== EXTRACTION RULES ===
- If the customer changes their claim during the conversation, use the FINAL version.
- If the customer describes damage that maps to multiple issue types (e.g., "scratched and dented"), pick the PRIMARY or most severe one for claimed_issue_type. Include all in damage_keywords.
- If the customer mentions a part name that is not in the allowed list, map it to the closest match. If no reasonable match exists, use "unknown".
- Do NOT infer damage that the customer did not describe. Only extract what they actually claim.
- If the conversation contains no clear damage claim at all, use claimed_issue_type: "none", claimed_object_part: "unknown", and empty damage_keywords.

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===
Respond with ONLY a valid JSON object, no additional text or explanation:

```json
{
  "claimed_issue_type": "<one of the allowed issue_type values>",
  "claimed_object_part": "<one of the allowed object_part values for the claim_object>",
  "damage_keywords": ["<keyword1>", "<keyword2>"],
  "claimed_severity_description": "<customer's severity description or 'not explicitly stated'>",
  "num_parts_claimed": <integer>
}
```
